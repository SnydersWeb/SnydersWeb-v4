    My Background


    Molly and I at Cascade Locks, OR
    I've almost always been a computer programmer. I started in the summer of my third grade year with a "Kids and the Apple" class that taught me the very basics and from that point on I was off and running. My forth grade teacher, Ron Stone, learned how to program by watching me. I can still vividly recall him telling my parents "You have got to get this kid a computer!" My mom and dad scrimped and saved - eventually purchasing our Franklin Ace 1200, essentially an Apple II Plus knockoff, where I spent countless hours playing, programming, and learning.

    After graduating from Hamilton Heights, I attended Purdue University in the department of Technical Graphics (now called Computer Graphics). During my student years my computer skills stood out a bit amongst my peers and department faculty would sometimes ask for my assistance in troubleshooting their computer issues. I took the very first HTML course the university offered and build some of my first web pages. The Web was the perfect place for me to I was able to combine my computer expertise, enjoyment of programming, and degree in graphics into a career that I have a love and passion for.

    My professional career has lead me to a few places, first staring for a company named Datastream Systems, Inc. based out of Greenville, SC in 1996 where I worked for 5 years as the company webmaster. While there I garnered several awards for my work and learned a number of skills I use to this day.

    After Datastream I moved to Colorado in 2002 where I took my next job in Gypsum, CO as webmaster for a company called WestStar Bank. While there I lived in the city of Glenwood Springs - famous for natural Hot Springs and the Glenwood Adventure park. During that time I volunteered my time and talents for the Glenwood Springs Center for the Arts designing their site seen here and designing posters for their main fundraising event - the Wine and Brewfest.

    WestStar Bank was eventually acquired by U.S. Bank and I went to work for a company called Wall Street On Demand based out of Boulder, CO in 2007 and moved to Thornton where I reside today.

    Working at Wall Street on Demand was an amazing experience. I knew when I was offered the job I would be taking a tiger by the tail, that it would be intense, intimidating, exciting and that I would be walking amongst giants there. I learned so much about the craft of web development - growing from a very competent developer to mastery of a great plethora of different technologies.

    In 2009 I met and married my wonderful wife, Molly. Five years later, we were joined by our son. Being a husband and father has been one of those few things I've enjoyed more than being a developer.

    After seventeen years and numerous acquisitions, I have parted ways with Wall Street On Demand/Markit/IHS Markit/S&P Global and am currently seeking the next chapter of my career.
